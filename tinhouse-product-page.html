The engine

It reads your story before it touches a single word.

Most tools process page by page — so chapter three doesn't know what happened in chapter one. Characters drift. Voice flattens. The output reads like an imitation of your book, not your book.

FluidScriptr works differently. Before it generates anything, it builds a Story Bible — a complete map of your characters, arcs, voice, and themes. Every line it produces is anchored to that map.

Think of it the way you'd think of a great adapter: someone who reads your entire manuscript, understands what makes it yours, and then translates it — faithfully — into a new form. That's not replacing your creativity. That's respecting it.

Common questions

Things writers ask.

Do I need a finished manuscript to use FluidScriptr?
No. FluidScriptr has three starting points: upload a manuscript, answer six story questions to generate one from an idea, or upload existing pages to enhance. You can start with nothing but an idea.
What is the Story Bible?
The Story Bible is a structured analysis of your story — characters, arcs, voice, tone, and themes — built before any output is generated. Every scene FluidScriptr writes is anchored to this map, so nothing drifts and your voice stays consistent from page one to the end.
Which output formats does FluidScriptr support?
Screenplays export in Fountain, Final Draft (.fdx), and formatted PDF. Manuscripts export as DOCX, PDF, and RTF — Times New Roman, double-spaced, one-inch margins — submission-ready from the first export.
Do you store or train on my manuscript?
No. FluidScriptr processes everything locally in your browser session. Your API key is stored only in your browser, uploads are not retained, and your work is never used for model training. What you write stays yours.
What AI model does FluidScriptr use?
FluidScriptr runs on Claude (Anthropic) via your own API key. You bring the key — FluidScriptr brings the workflow. This keeps your costs transparent, your data private, and your content off shared infrastructure. Typical usage costs a few cents per conversion.
